The Path to Depth

  1. Map the terrain.Skim 3–5 foundational sources. Don't study yet — just build an index of what exists.
  2. Find the 20% that repeats.Every niche has recurring problems. Watch the summary and papers for the same 2–3 tensions showing up again and again.
  3. Read the primary sources, not summaries.Use the papers panel. Pick one paper you understand end to end before moving to the next.
  4. Find who's active now.Not just canonical authors — who's publishing in the last 2 years. Follow their citation trail.
  5. Write one page in your own words.Explaining it to someone else is the fastest way to expose what you don't actually understand.
